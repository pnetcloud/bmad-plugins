# Comparison And Alternative Pages

Use this file when building or reviewing pages targeting competitor or alternative queries.

## Goal

Create comparison surfaces that genuinely help evaluators choose, instead of publishing thin doorway pages that repeat competitor names without substance.

## A Comparison Page Deserves To Exist When

- the query has real evaluation intent
- the page can explain differences honestly
- the page can show who each option fits best
- the page can route the visitor to proof, migration help, pricing, or product detail

Do not build the page if the team cannot say something specific and credible.

## Required Page Shape

Strong comparison pages usually include:

- short verdict or TL;DR
- comparison table with real decision criteria
- strengths and tradeoffs for both sides
- fit guidance by persona or use case
- migration or switching path
- proof links and CTA

## Honesty Rules

- acknowledge when the competitor is stronger in some dimensions
- do not invent features, pricing details, or claims
- do not frame every difference as a win if the evidence does not support it

Credibility matters more than over-optimization.

## Internal Linking Expectations

- inbound from competitor or alternatives hubs
- outbound to pricing, proof, feature pages, migration docs, and onboarding help
- links should help a user verify claims and take the next decision step

## Schema Suggestions

- `Article`
- `BreadcrumbList`
- `FAQPage` only when FAQs are visible, useful, and not filler

## When To Noindex Or Retire

Consider noindex, merge, or removal when:

- the page is thin and repetitive
- there is no evidence or differentiated guidance
- competitor coverage is outdated and cannot be maintained
- dozens of pages exist with only brand-name swaps

## Review Questions

- Does the page make a decision easier?
- Are the tradeoffs honest?
- Does the page show proof or only claims?
- Is there a useful next step after the comparison?
